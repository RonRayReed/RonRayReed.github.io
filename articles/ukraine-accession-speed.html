Ukraine accession speed vs. Moldova: why Chișinău may close the gap faster

Both Ukraine and Moldova applied for EU candidate status in 2022 and were granted it simultaneously. Reed Research examines why Moldova — smaller, less complex, and without an active conflict — may complete the accession process before Ukraine despite Ukraine's larger political profile.

Reed Research DeskReed Intelligence Group
Published22 April 2026
Read time5 min

Ukraine and Moldova submitted EU membership applications on the same day in March 2022 and received candidate status simultaneously in June 2022. Four years on, their accession trajectories have diverged in ways that were not anticipated at the outset.

Ukraine has opened more negotiation clusters — its size, geopolitical importance, and intense diplomatic investment mean the EU has prioritized its accession process formally. But Moldova has moved faster in per-chapter terms, closing provisionally two screening chapters that Ukraine has not yet completed.

Why size is a disadvantage in accession

EU accession requires comprehensive alignment of a candidate's entire legal, regulatory, and institutional framework with the EU acquis communautaire — a body of law covering approximately 80,000 pages. For a country of 43 million people with 25 regulatory ministries, this alignment requires a scale of institutional capacity that Moldova — population 2.6 million — does not need to match.

Moldova's smaller economy means fewer sectors require accession-related legislative overhaul. Moldova's financial sector, for example, has three significant banks versus Ukraine's twenty-plus. Its agricultural sector has far fewer geographic and product-type classifications requiring alignment with EU food safety standards.

The conflict variable

Ukraine's active conflict introduces an accession variable that has no precedent in EU enlargement history. No country has acceded to the EU while engaged in active armed conflict on its territory. The EU treaties do not explicitly prohibit it, but practical obstacles — particularly around security sector alignment and territorial integrity requirements — make accession during active conflict legally complex. Moldova has no such complication.
