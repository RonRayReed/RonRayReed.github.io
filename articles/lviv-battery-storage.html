USAID funds battery storage pilot for Lviv energy grid resilience

USAID's Ukraine Energy Security Project has funded a battery energy storage system pilot for the Lviv regional distribution network. The 10 MWh system, supplied by a US manufacturer, entered service in March 2026.

Reed Intel DeskLviv Business Intel
PublishedMarch 2026
Read time4 min

USAID's Ukraine Energy Security Project funded the installation of a 10 megawatt-hour battery energy storage system (BESS) for Lviv's regional electricity distribution network. The system, supplied by US manufacturer Powin Energy, entered service in March 2026. It is the first utility-scale BESS installation in western Ukraine.

The system is located at a distribution substation serving the Sykhiv residential district and Lviv's western industrial zone. It is designed to provide 90 minutes of backup power during grid outages — a critical function during Russian missile and drone attacks that periodically target Ukraine's energy infrastructure.

USAID's Ukraine Energy Security Project has committed $520 million to Ukrainian energy resilience since 2022. The Lviv BESS pilot is one of six distributed storage projects funded under the program's 2025–2026 cycle.
