Kernel redirects grain flows to Chornomorsk as war-risk insurance costs rise

Kernel, Ukraine's largest sunflower oil producer and grain exporter, has increased the proportion of exports routed through Chornomorsk following a favorable adjustment in war-risk insurance terms for the terminal's dedicated vessel program.

Reed Intel DeskOdesa Business Intel
PublishedApril 2026
Read time4 min

Kernel, Ukraine's largest agribusiness by revenue and the country's leading sunflower oil exporter, has increased its utilization of the Chornomorsk terminal following a war-risk insurance adjustment that improved economics for direct Black Sea routing. The company confirmed the routing shift in its Q1 2026 trading update.

Kernel operates the largest private grain terminal at Chornomorsk, with an annual throughput capacity of approximately 9 million tonnes. The terminal has its own war-risk insurance arrangement under a dedicated fleet program, which secured more favorable premium rates than open-market spot insurance for individual voyage cover.

The routing shift has a direct impact on Odesa and Danube port volumes. Kernel had been directing a larger share of flows through Reni and Izmail during periods when Black Sea war-risk costs peaked in 2024–2025. The return of Kernel volume to Chornomorsk contributes to the port cluster's 2025 throughput record.
