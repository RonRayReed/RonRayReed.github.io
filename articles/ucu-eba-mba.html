UCU and EBA launch first EU-accredited MBA in western Ukraine

Ukrainian Catholic University and the European Business Association have jointly launched an EU-accredited MBA program in Lviv — the first in western Ukraine. The two-year program begins in September 2026 with an initial cohort of 40 students.

Reed Intel DeskLviv Business Intel
PublishedMarch 2026
Read time4 min

Ukrainian Catholic University (UCU) and the European Business Association (EBA) announced a joint MBA program in March 2026 that carries EU-recognized accreditation through a partnership with an Austrian university partner. The program is the first EU-accredited graduate business degree offered in western Ukraine. It begins in September 2026 with an initial cohort of 40 students.

The program is designed for working professionals and runs on a modular format over two years, with intensive residential weeks held alternately in Lviv and Vienna. Tuition is set at €12,000 for the full program. UCU confirmed that 15 partial scholarships funded by corporate EBA members are available for the inaugural cohort.
